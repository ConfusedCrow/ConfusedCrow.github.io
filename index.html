Rustbucket VR (2017)

Rustbucket VR was a small VR Coop Shooter/Racing-Game I developed as part of a small team for one of my courses. RustbucketVR is played in VR and on a regular monitor simultaneously. Inspired by the "Mad Max"-Franchise, both players take to the desert streets in their trusty pickup-truck, the "Rustbucket". The PC-player is tasked with driving the car, dodging bullets and bombs, as well as picking up power-ups for the VR player to use. Meanwhile, the VR-Player is tasked with protecting the car from getting damaged by the various enemies scattered across the canyon. To do this, the VR-Player has access to a wide array of different weapons, ranging from a simple weapon to sniper rifles and rocket launchers.
During the project, I was responsible for all of the interactions in VR, all of the interactions between the player, as well as menu,tutorial, and customization implementations. A video demonstrating all of the features can be found here.

Senior Mutant Ninja Scientists (2019)

Senior Mutant Ninja Scientists was a project I worked on as part of the "Trier GameDevWeek"-Gamejam in 2019.
SMNS is a small local-multiplayer game in which 3 scientists try to mutate each other with various potions. Every time a scientist or a tile on the ground is hit, another effect gets applied to them, making things easier or harder, depending on the potion used. Active effects are visualized by different cosmetic changes.
You can watch a presentation, detailing all the features, here.

Save_Memory (2018)

Saving_Memory was a first-person shooter I developed as part of a small team for one of my courses. In this small tech-demo, the player is tasked with escaping an abandoned high-tech bunker using various weapons and special abilities, such as localised time-stops and telekinesis. The player was also give the ability to craft their own weapons at dedicated workbenches. During the development of Save_Memory, I was in charge of the player movement, quest system, and the implementation of all the special abilities.

GalaxyAttack (2018)

Galaxy Attack was a multiplayer AR mobile game I developed for a friends design-course. In Galaxy Attack, competing players are tasked with protecting their base from attacking spaceships. During gameplay, the players scan the room and place their crystal at a convenient position. Once every player has placed their base and the location synchronization between them is completed, spaceships, emerging from randomly placed portals, begin to attack. Enemies could appear in many different forms. Some teleported, some flew and some clustered around a Mothership, supporting its damage and health. Different colors of enemies were vulnerable to different weapons. All of the code was written in unity in combination with Apple's AR Framework.

Sword Dash (2020 | WIP)

Sword Dash is a small endless runner for Android devices I originally created for a personal 5 day development challenge, but will iterate on in the future. In Sword Dash, the player must traverse an endless dungeon corridor filled with traps and dangerous enemies. The game is controlled by swiping on the display. Swipes on the left side control the movement while the right side is used for blocking and attacking. The enemies scattered across are equipped with swords and can only be defeated by parrying their attacks. To successfully parry, the player needs to swipe in the correct blocking direction, inside a tight timeframe, before getting hit by an enemies strike. If the player blocks too early, the enemy merely gets pushed back and resumes its attack. The player is also able to change his health, speed buildup and parry window by equipping different pieces of armor and weapons.
A github repository containing all of the code can be found here.

Health GameJam Labyrinth

This game was developed in 3 days as part of the "Health GameJam 2019". The goal was to create a game which can be enjoyed by all players regardless of disability. We chose to create a game in which 3 players must work together to escape a randomly generated maze. Each player is in charge of one of the senses (Sight, Hearing, Touch) and may only perceive one direction (Left, Right, Up). In each of their turns, the players attempt to navigate the maze, by using their own information as well as remembering those of their teammates. Their turn ends once they run into a wall. The whole team wins once one player reaches the exit.
